Places worth visiting in Odisha:-

Puri,Odisha

Konark Sun Temple

On the shores of the Bay of Bengal, bathed in the rays of the rising sun, the temple at Konark a.k.a The Black Pagoda is a monumental representation of the sun god Surya's chariot.

Puri, Odisha

Puri Konark Marine Drive

Puri Konark marine drive lends that beautiful encounter with sea on one side and coastal forest on the other side and in between there runs a smooth road with almost zero traffic.

Puri, Odisha

Jagannath Temple

The Jagannath Temple at Puri, one of the four Char Dham is one of the most revered Vaishnava sites of worship in India. One of the oldest Hindu temples to still be in use.

Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Dhauli Giri

The Dhauli Shanti Stupa or the Peace Pagoda is built on the site where Kalinga War took place and it commemorates the mission of peace that the Kalinga War achieved.

Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Ekamra Kshetra

Ekamra Kshetra or the temple city of Bhubaneswar is a series of ancient sandstone temples, heritage ponds and water tanks. The temples are built in the style of Kalinga architecture.

Puri, Odisha

Golden Beach

Puri Beach or the Golden beach is a beach in the city of Puri in the state of Odisha, India. It is on the shore of the Bay of Bengal. It is known for being a tourist attraction and a Hindu sacred place.

Kendrapara, Odisha

Bhitarkanika National Park

Bhitarkanika is the 2nd largest Mangrove ecosystems of India and has one of the largest population of endangered saltwater crocodile. Nearly 1671 saltwater crocodiles inhabit the rivers and creeks.

Balasore, Odisha

Chandipur Sea Beach

The Chandipur coast is known for its ‘Vanishing Sea’ phenomenon. Here one can the sea recedes by almost 5 to 6 kilometres everyday during low tide and then comes back at high tide.

Puri, Khurdha and Ganjam, Odisha

Chilika Lake

Chilika Lake is the 2nd largest lake in India and the largest coastal lagoon in India and also the second largest brackish water lagoon in the world. It is the largest wintering ground for migratory birds on the Indian sub-continent

Koraput, Odisha

Duduma Waterfalls

The 175-metre high Duduma waterfall tears through the rugged rocks of the Eastern Ghats and the dark green deciduous forest that is so widespread in this region.It is located on the border of Odisha and Andhra pradesh.

Sambalpur, Odisha

Hirakud Dam

Hirakud Dam is the longest earthen dam in the world. The length of the total dam is 25.8 km while the length of the main dam is 4.8 km. The mjaestic dam drains an area of 1,33,090 Sq.Kms., more than twice the area of Sri lanka

Gajapati, Odisha

Jirang Monastery

Jirang Monastery or the Padmasambhava Mahavihara Monastery is said to be the largest Buddhist monastery in Eastern India. Jirang, Odisha’s Little Tibet offered the Tibetan settler’s a home after China invaded Tibet in 1959.

Ganjam, Odisha

Kalijai Island, Chilika

Kalijai Temple is located on an island in Chilika Lake. It is considered to be the abode of the Goddess Kalijai. Goddess Kalijai is actually Kali, the source and the first 'Mahavidya' of 'Dasmahavidyas'. The deity has been venerated in the local folklores and legends

Jagatsinghpur, Odisha

Paradip Sea Port

Paradip Port is a natural, deep-water port built by late Chief Minister of Odisha Biju Pattnaik on the East coast of India in Paradip. It is one of the largest port on India's east coast and first major port in the East Coast commissioned after independence.

Jajpur, Odisha

Ratnagiri

Ratnagiri was the birthplace of a tantric form of Buddhism known as Vajrayana . This was Odisha’s Nalanda University and large number of Buddhist scholars from all over the world came to study at Ratnagiri. Ratnagiri is the only monastery in India to have a curvilinear roof.

Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Khandagiri and Udaygiri

It is believed that most of these caves were carved out as residential blocks for Jain monks during the reign of Jain Emperor Kharavela of the Kalinga Empire. Archaeological Survey of India has listed Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves in the list of "Must See" Indian Heritage.
